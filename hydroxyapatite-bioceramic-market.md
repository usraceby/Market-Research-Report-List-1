<p><p>Hydroxyapatite bioceramics are categorized into four types based on their form and applications. Granular forms are often used in bone grafting to promote healing and integration. Massive hydroxyapatite implants provide structural support for various orthopedic applications. Flaky hydroxyapatite is utilized for coating implants, enhancing biocompatibility and promoting osseointegration. Powdered hydroxyapatite is frequently used in dental applications and as a filler material due to its high surface area, facilitating interactions with biological tissues. Each type serves specialized roles in medical and dental fields.</p></p>
