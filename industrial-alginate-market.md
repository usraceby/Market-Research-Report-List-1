<p><p>The industrial alginate market is categorized into two main types: High G and Low G alginates. High G alginates, derived primarily from species like Macrocystis, have a high g Guluronic acid content, leading to stronger gels and enhanced stability, making them suitable for applications in food, pharmaceuticals, and textiles. Low G alginates, predominantly from species such as Laminaria, contain higher proportions of mannuronic acid, resulting in softer gels, ideally used in personal care, coatings, and as thickening agents in various industries.</p></p>
