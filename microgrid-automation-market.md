<p><p>Microgrid automation encompasses various types, including fuel cells, combined heat and power (CHP) systems, and others. Fuel cells generate electrical energy through electrochemical reactions, offering high efficiency and low emissions. CHP systems simultaneously produce electricity and useful heat from a single fuel source, optimizing energy use. Other technologies in microgrid automation may include solar photovoltaic systems, batteries, and wind turbines, each contributing to enhanced energy management, reliability, and sustainability in localized energy systems. Together, they facilitate flexible, resilient energy solutions.</p></p>
