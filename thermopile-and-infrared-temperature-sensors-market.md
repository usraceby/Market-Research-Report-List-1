<p><p>Thermopile and infrared temperature sensors are devices used to measure temperature from a distance without needing direct contact with the target. Thermopiles consist of multiple thermocouples that convert thermal energy into electrical voltage, while infrared sensors detect infrared radiation emitted by objects to determine their temperature. These sensors are widely utilized in various applications, including industrial automation, medical devices, HVAC systems, and consumer electronics due to their precision, reliability, and non-contact measurement capabilities.</p><p>The market for thermopile and infrared temperature sensors is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for non-invasive and accurate temperature measurement solutions in various sectors. Through Hole Type sensors are designed for traditional PCB mounting, providing robust mechanical support and thermal performance, suitable for various industrial applications. In contrast, SMD Type sensors offer a compact, space-saving design for high-density electronic assemblies.
