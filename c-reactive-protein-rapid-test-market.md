<p><p>The C-Reactive Protein (CRP) Rapid Test market includes various testing methods. The immunoturbidimetric assay measures the cloudiness in a solution due to CRP-antibody complexes. ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) utilizes enzyme reactions to quantify CRP levels. Chemiluminescence immunoassay (CLIA) employs light emission for highly sensitive detection of CRP. Other methods may include lateral flow assays or point-of-care tests that provide rapid results. Each type caters to different clinical needs, sensitivity, and specificity requirements in monitoring inflammation.</p></p>
